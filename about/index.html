    About

    I’m a software engineer at MathWorks. More about my professional life on LinkedIn.

    In December of 2020 I completed a Computer Science master’s degree at Florida Atlantic University, focusing on machine learning. The thesis is available here. I’m now a Ph.D. candidate, focusing on machine learning, continuing the work I did for the master’s degree. My master’s and Ph.D. projects are on GitHub.

    Publications

    • Master’s thesis.
    • Code for assignments, research projects, and blog posts are on GitHub.

    Papers and articles where I’m the main author:

    • Machine learning for predicting opioid use disorder from healthcare data, a systematic review. The accepted manuscript is available here (free to access).
    • Assessing Methods and Tools to Improve Reporting, Increase Transparency, and Reduce Failures in Machine Learning Applications in Health Care, on the RSNA Radiology:Artificial Intelligence journal (free version available on the NIH Library of Medicine site). This article is based on the master’s thesis.
    • Dataset datasheet for CheXpert, in collaboration with the Stanford CheXpert team.
    • Dropout vs. batch normalization. The free version of the paper is available here and the code is on GitHub.

    Papers and articles where I’m a collaborator:

    • Automated patient localization in challenging hospital environments.
    • Deep learning for edge devices.
    • On the use of colormaps in the context of post-hoc explainable AI methods, an abstract session in SIIM 2022.
    • IEEE ICMLA data science tutorial notebooks, the practical part of Dr. Marques’ The Data Science landscape: foundations, tools, and practical applications session.

    More publications and patents on Google Scholar.
